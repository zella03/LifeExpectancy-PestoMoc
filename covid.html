The Pandemic That Changed the World

Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. First identified in late 2019, it rapidly evolved into a global pandemic, disrupting lives, economies, and healthcare systems. Characterized by symptoms ranging from mild coughs to severe respiratory distress, the virus has profoundly impacted public health and highlighted the importance of global collaboration in combating emerging diseases.

Deaths due to covid

This map visualizes COVID-19 deaths per 100,000 people as of February 15, 2024. Darker shades indicate higher death rates, revealing significant geographic disparities. Peru exhibits the highest death rate, while regions in Africa and parts of Asia report lower rates. The data highlights the uneven global impact of the pandemic, shaped by healthcare access, population demographics, and pandemic management strategies.

Biggest life expectancy decrease

This line chart shows the largest decreases in life expectancy globally between 2019 and 2020. Even among the countries most severely affected, the declines vary significantly, with Bolivia experiencing the steepest drop and Andorra the smallest. While the COVID-19 pandemic is likely a primary factor, other contributing factors may have played a role in these varying declines. This highlights that decreases in life expectancy are not solely correlated with deaths per 100k people from a single cause, but rather a complex interplay of factors impacting overall mortality.
