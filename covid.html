The Pandemic That Changed the World

Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. First identified in late 2019, it rapidly evolved into a global pandemic, disrupting lives, economies, and healthcare systems.
Characterized by symptoms ranging from mild coughs to severe respiratory distress, the virus has profoundly impacted public health and highlighted the importance of global collaboration in combating emerging diseases.

Life Expectancy Drops Significantly After COVID

From years, we clearly observed a steady increase in life expectancy, as people across Europe were living longer year by year. Unfortunately, this positive trend was interrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Andorra experienced the highest decline in life expectancy, with a drop of -4.68 years between 2019 and 2020. This means that, during this period, people in Andorra were expected to live nearly 5 years shorter than before the pandemic hit. Many countries have quickly recovered, returning to pre-pandemic life expectancy levels and resuming the upward trend.

How to Read the Orthographic Map?

This map visualizes the total confirmed COVID-19 deaths per 100,000 people as of February 15, 2024 across various countries.

  • Darker shades indicate higher death rates.
  • Hover over a country to see specific data for that region.
  • Use the legend on the side to understand the color scale and compare countries.
  • Zoom and pan to explore detailed data for smaller regions.

How to Read the Chart?

The chart visualizes life expectancy trends for countries with the highest decrease during covid pandemic.

  • The X-axis represents the years, while the Y-axis shows life expectancy values.
  • Each line represents a different country, with its color corresponding to a specific country.
  • Hover over a line to see detailed information about a country, including life expectancy for 2019 and 2020, and the difference between those years.
  • Dots on the chart represent life expectancy values for specific years (2019 and 2020).
