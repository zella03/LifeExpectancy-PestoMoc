How to read the map?

Western Europe Dominance

Spanning the years 2000-2021 it becomes evident that most of the countries with high healthcare expenditure per capita (Germany, Norway, Switzerland) are located in the western part of Europe.

In recent years, Switzerland stands out with the highest per capita expenditure. We should know that per capita expenditres are more reliable than total expenditures as they reflect the amount spent relative to each population unit, correlating to better life quality of individuals and individuals well-being. When switching to total expenditures, the map reveals that wealthier nations not only have higher per capita spending but also dominate in healthcare expenditure.

What is good for us - we can observe that total healthcare expenditures are increasing across all countries over the years!

How to read the chart?

We are living longer, longer, and longer...

Life expectancy improvements often correlate with healthcare spending increases. We can conclude that there is a small but positive relationship between those two values!

You remember the countries we compared at the end of GDP section? Looking at the Russian Federation and the United Kingdom, we can observe something interesting. Over the same time period, both countries saw an increase in healthcare spending. However, despite spending less on healthcare, Russia experienced a significantly higher rate of growth in life expectancy compared to the UK.

In contrast, the United Kingdom, which is actually allocating more resources to healthcare, saw a relatively smaller improvement in life expectancy during the same time period!
It can be seen that we face overall increase in many european countries in healthcare spending. Given this correlation, we are going to live longer!

How to Read the Choropleth Map?

The choropleth map visualizes healthcare expenditures across countries:

  • Hover over a country to see detailed data for the selected year.
  • Use the year selector to view data for different years.
  • Switch between per capita and total expenditure using the dropdown menu.
  • Darker colors represent higher expenditures.

Preparation Activities
Life Expectancy data was transformed into a long format, we renamed the columns, extracted the years and exported into yearly subsets to CSV files.

Healthcare dataset was reshaped it to long format using, extracted years, dropped rows with missing values, and converted healthcare expenditure from USD to EUR using a conversion rate (0.97).

Datasets were loaded and merged together on country and year. Additinally total healthcare expenditure was calculated (by multiplication using population), and then again saved to yearly datasets.

To display countries on a map some of the countries names needed to be standardized to the ones from geojson file.
